So what exactly is the greenhouse effect?

Sunlight makes the Earth habitable. While 30% of the solar energy that reaches our world is reflected back to space, only 70% gets passed through Earth's atmosphere where it gets trapped by the land, oceans, and atmosphere, and heats up the planet. This heat is then radiated back in the form of invisible infrared light. While some of this light continues on into space, the vast majority-indeed, some 90 percent-gets absorbed by atmospheric gases and redirected back towards the Earth; causing further warming. Because how these gases warm up our planet, they are called as greenhouse gases and the effect they create in the atmosphere is called the greenhouse effect.

But you might ask "If this is a natural phenomena, then why is everyone making such a big deal out of it?"

Although it is natural, the fact that we are contributing to the greenhouse gases in the atmosphere by burning fossil fuels at such a great rate means that we are making the greenhouse effect worse, which is having terrible consequences on life on Earth.

For instance, an increase in the atmospheric concentrations of greenhouse gases produces a positive climate forcing, or warming effect. From 1990 to 2019, the total warming effects from greenhouse gases added by humans to the Earth's atmosphere increased by 45 percent.

They also contribute to respiratory diseases from smog and air pollution. Extreme weather, food supply disruptions, and increased wildfires are other effects of climate change caused by greenhouse gases.

When there is a will, there is a way. So below I will provide two wonderful methods to reduce greenhouse gases.

Plant more trees

Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and, in turn, give off oxygen. One tree absorbs around one ton of carbon dioxide in the whole of its life time.

Drive smart and less

If people are to drive less, it means fewer carbon dioxide emission because people will save on gasoline. Driving green saves money and reduces carbon footprint.
